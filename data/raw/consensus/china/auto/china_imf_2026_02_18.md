title: IMF Executive Board Concludes 2025 Article IV Consultation with China

The IMF described China's economy as resilient in 2025 because exports and policy stimulus held growth near target, but it also emphasized that private domestic demand remained weak and deflationary pressures persisted. The release pointed to slower growth in 2026, muted inflation, and downside risks tied to property weakness and elevated debt. It argued that more expansionary macro support, including further monetary easing, may still be needed while China rebalances toward consumption-led growth.
